How to Set Up Kovaak’s FPS Aim Trainer

Written by Coach Moby - N1 Esports


Press ESC to open the game menu

Sensitivity

Click Settings > Select your game in Sensitivity Scale > Input your sensitivity in Horizontal Sensitivity

Field of View (FOV)

Select your game in FOV Measurements

  • Fortnite: 80
  • Valorant: 103
  • Overwatch: 103

Max FPS

Set your max FPS to the refresh rate of your monitor to start. You can decrease this if you are unable to maintain that FPS or if you want to train at a lower FPS to adapt to situations where you may be playing on lower FPS (ex. Fortnite end game).

Statistics Export:

This is very important if you want to utilize our analytics tool (hyperlink) - you must make sure this is not set to “None”. Set this to export whatever data points you would like to analyze - this can be challenge completion (disregards any data when you refresh a scenario), any challenge (saves data anytime you start a challenge), scenarios/challenges or always.

Keybinds:

Set your keybinds to match whatever game you are practicing for. Generally you can keep these default.

Weapons:

I recommend hiding your weapon here so it does not block any of your targets. Customize your crosshair in this tab to match your crosshair in your main game.

Video Settings:

Set your resolution to match the resolution of the game you are practicing for.

V-Sync: OFF
Anti-Aliasing: NO AA
Post Processing: OFF
Shadows: OFF
Effects: OFF
One Frame Thread Lag: OFF
Anisotropic Filter: x0
Scene Color: OFF
Gamma: 2.2
Simulated Input Lag: 0

Visual Settings:

I recommend you play with these settings until you find colors and textures that you enjoy playing with. You can attempt to emulate your game here but I would recommend selecting simple colors and textures as to not distract from your training.

Sound Settings:

The new default sounds as of the latest patch are alpha and I recommend leaving this unchanged.

UI Settings:

Use this tab to hide anything you don’t want on your screen while practicing. I recommend hiding everything if you are likely to be distracted. You can leave session stats while in practice mode to access how you are doing and challenge timer during challenges.
